1.3.1 Introduction to Density Functionals

The many-electron wavefunction \( \Psi(\vec{r}_1 \sigma_1, \ldots, \vec{r}_N \sigma_N) \) contains tremendous detail, but is rarely used directly. It‚Äôs a high-dimensional object, making it difficult to compute, interpret, or even store. In practice, we often prefer to work with simpler quantities such as the electron densities \( n_\uparrow(\vec{r}) \) and \( n_\downarrow(\vec{r}) \), especially for the ground state.

To make this shift, we introduce the concept of density functionals: instead of using the wavefunction \( \Psi \), we express physical quantities as functionals of the electron density \( n(\vec{r}) \).

üìê Functionals and Functional Derivatives

A function maps numbers to numbers: \( f(x) \rightarrow y \). A functional maps functions to numbers: \( F[n] \rightarrow y \). For example, the exchange energy in the local density approximation (LDA) is given by:

\[ E_x^{\mathrm{LDA}}[n] = A_x \int d^3r \; n(\vec{r})^{4/3} \tag{1.49} \]

To understand how a functional changes when \( n(\vec{r}) \) varies slightly, we introduce the functional derivative:

\[ \delta F = \int d^3r \left( \frac{\delta F}{\delta n(\vec{r})} \right) \delta n(\vec{r}) \tag{1.50} \]

Applying this to the LDA exchange energy gives:

\[ \delta E_x^{\mathrm{LDA}} = A_x \int d^3r \left[ (n(\vec{r}) + \delta n(\vec{r}))^{4/3} - n(\vec{r})^{4/3} \right] = A_x \int d^3r \; \frac{4}{3} n(\vec{r})^{1/3} \delta n(\vec{r}) \tag{1.50'} \]

Therefore, the functional derivative is:

\[ \frac{\delta E_x^{\mathrm{LDA}}}{\delta n(\vec{r})} = A_x \frac{4}{3} n(\vec{r})^{1/3} \tag{1.51} \]

Similarly, the Hartree (classical Coulomb) energy is given by:

\[ U[n] = \frac{1}{2} \int d^3r \int d^3r' \frac{n(\vec{r}) n(\vec{r}')}{|\vec{r} - \vec{r}'|} \]

Its functional derivative gives the Hartree potential:

\[ \frac{\delta U[n]}{\delta n(\vec{r})} = u[n](\vec{r}) \tag{1.52} \]

where \( u[n](\vec{r}) \) is the classical electrostatic potential generated by \( n(\vec{r}) \). This derivative arises naturally when we minimize the total energy functional.
