1.3.2 Density Variational Principle

We aim to derive a density-functional analog of the wavefunction variational principle (Eq. (1.30)). Unlike the traditional proof of Hohenberg–Kohn theorem by contradiction, this approach uses a constructive framework.

From the variational principle:

\[ E = \min_{\Psi} \langle \Psi | \hat{H} | \Psi \rangle \tag{1.53} \]

We restrict this minimization to all antisymmetric N-particle wavefunctions \( \Psi \).

Now, we divide the optimization in two steps. First, fix a density \( n(\vec{r}) \). All wavefunctions that yield this same density form a subset. Within that subset, minimize the energy:

\[ \min_{\Psi \to n} \langle \Psi | \hat{H} | \Psi \rangle = \min_{\Psi \to n} \langle \Psi | \hat{T} + \hat{V}_{ee} | \Psi \rangle + \int d^3r\, n(\vec{r})v(\vec{r}) \tag{1.54} \]

Define the universal functional:

\[ F[n] = \min_{\Psi \to n} \langle \Psi | \hat{T} + \hat{V}_{ee} | \Psi \rangle \tag{1.55} \]

Then, the full variational principle becomes:

\[ E = \min_n \left[ F[n] + \int d^3r\, v(\vec{r}) n(\vec{r}) \right] \tag{1.56} \]

The density that minimizes this expression is the ground-state density. To ensure particle number conservation, we introduce a Lagrange multiplier \( \mu \):

\[ \delta \left\{ F[n] + \int d^3r\, v(\vec{r})n(\vec{r}) - \mu \int d^3r\, n(\vec{r}) \right\} = 0 \tag{1.57} \]

This leads to the Euler–Lagrange equation:

\[ \frac{\delta F[n]}{\delta n(\vec{r})} + v(\vec{r}) = \mu \tag{1.58} \]

This equation determines the ground-state density. The external potential \( v(\vec{r}) \) is uniquely determined by the ground-state density, up to a constant. Thus, the functional \( F[n] \) is universal.

In spin-dependent systems, the same logic applies to spin densities \( n_\uparrow(\vec{r}) \) and \( n_\downarrow(\vec{r}) \). Then, the Euler equation generalizes, and the minimization is done with both densities fixed separately.
