1.3.3 Kohn–Sham Non-interacting System

For a system of non-interacting electrons, the electron-electron repulsion \( \hat{V}_{ee} \) vanishes, so \( F[n] \) reduces to:

\[ T_s[n] = \min_{\Psi \rightarrow n} \langle \Psi | \hat{T} | \Psi \rangle = \langle \Psi^{\text{min}}_s | \hat{T} | \Psi^{\text{min}}_s \rangle \tag{1.59} \]

Although we could search over all antisymmetric \( N \)-electron wavefunctions as in Eq. (1.54), the minimizing wavefunction \( \Psi^{\text{min}}_s \) will be non-interacting — a single Slater determinant or linear combination of a few — for some potential \( v_s(\vec{r}) \).

The corresponding Euler equation becomes:

\[ \frac{\delta T_s[n]}{\delta n(\vec{r})} + v_s(\vec{r}) = \mu \tag{1.60} \]

Just as in Eq. (1.58). Hence, \( v_s(\vec{r}) \) is a functional of \( n(\vec{r}) \). If \( \mu \) and \( \mu_s \) differed, we could absorb it into \( v_s \). So we assume \( n(\vec{r}) \) is both interacting and non-interacting \( v \)-representable.

Now we define the exchange–correlation energy \( E_{xc}[n] \) via:

\[ F[n] = T_s[n] + U[n] + E_{xc}[n] \tag{1.61} \]

where \( U[n] \) is the classical Coulomb repulsion (as defined earlier). Now the Euler equations (1.58) and (1.60) yield:

\[ v_s(\vec{r}) = v(\vec{r}) + \frac{\delta U[n]}{\delta n(\vec{r})} + \frac{\delta E_{xc}[n]}{\delta n(\vec{r})} \tag{1.62} \]

So we recover the Kohn–Sham potential, matching Eq. (1.19) of Sect. 1.1.2.

Here, \( T_s[n] \) is exact, and all approximation lies in \( E_{xc}[n] \). This is reasonable because:

  • \( T_s[n] \) is the dominant part of \( F[n] \), while \( E_{xc}[n] \) is small
  • \( T_s[n] \) causes oscillations due to shell structure, which approximate functionals often fail to capture
  • \( T_s[n] \) is easily captured in Kohn–Sham approach

The total energy can now be written as:

\[ E = \sum_{i=1}^N \varepsilon_i - \int d^3r\, n(\vec{r}) v_{xc}[n](\vec{r}) + E_{xc}[n] \tag{1.63} \]

This expression accounts for overcounting of exchange-correlation energy in \( \sum \varepsilon_i \) and subtracts it out, adding back \( E_{xc}[n] \).
