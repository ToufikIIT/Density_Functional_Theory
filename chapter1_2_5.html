1.2.5 Virial Theorem

The virial theorem connects kinetic and potential energy under scaling. Consider a scaled wavefunction \( \Psi_\gamma(\vec{r}_1, ..., \vec{r}_N) = \gamma^{3N/2} \Psi(\gamma \vec{r}_1, ..., \gamma \vec{r}_N) \):

\[ \langle \Psi_\gamma | \Psi_\gamma \rangle = 1 \tag{1.39} \]

The scaled density becomes:

\[ n_\gamma(\vec{r}) = \gamma^3 n(\gamma \vec{r}) \tag{1.40} \]
\[ \int d^3r \; n_\gamma(\vec{r}) = N \tag{1.41} \]

By minimizing \( \langle T + V \rangle \) with respect to \( \gamma \), we obtain:

\[ \left. \frac{d}{d\gamma} \langle \Psi_\gamma | \hat{T} + \hat{V} | \Psi_\gamma \rangle \right|_{\gamma=1} = 0 \tag{1.42} \]

Since \( \hat{T} \sim \gamma^2 \):

\[ \langle \Psi_\gamma | \hat{T} | \Psi_\gamma \rangle = \gamma^2 \langle \Psi | \hat{T} | \Psi \rangle \tag{1.43} \]
\[ 2 \langle \hat{T} \rangle + \left. \frac{d}{d\gamma} \langle \hat{V} \rangle \right|_{\gamma=1} = 0 \tag{1.44} \]
\[ 2 \langle \hat{T} \rangle - \sum_{i=1}^N \left\langle \vec{r}_i \cdot \nabla_i \hat{V} \right\rangle = 0 \tag{1.45} \]

If \( \hat{V} \sim \gamma^n \), then

\[ \langle \hat{V}_\gamma \rangle = \gamma^n \langle \hat{V} \rangle \tag{1.47} \]
\[ 2 \langle \hat{T} \rangle - n \langle \hat{V} \rangle = 0 \tag{1.48} \]

For Coulombic systems (e.g. Eq. (1.36)), \( n = -1 \), yielding:

\[ 2 \langle \hat{T} \rangle + \langle \hat{V} \rangle = 0 \]

This relation helps derive energy expressions and test density approximations. When Hellmann–Feynman forces vanish (Eq. 1.37), the system is at equilibrium, and this virial relation becomes exact.
